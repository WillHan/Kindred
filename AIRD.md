# AIRD — what it is, and what actually helps

> ## You don't have to speak to be heard.

If you found this because AI is threatening your job and you feel anxious, lost, or like you're "no longer needed" — you're not broken, and you're not alone. There's a name for this now.

## What is AIRD?
**AIRD — AI Replacement Dysfunction** — is a term coined in 2025 by University of Florida researchers (Stephanie McNamara & Joseph Thornton) to describe the stress, anxiety, and loss of identity people feel as AI replaces or threatens their work. A key finding: **the strain doesn't require actually losing your job** — the fear and uncertainty are enough.

So if you're feeling it: it's real, it's been named, and a lot of people are feeling it with you.

## What it can feel like
Different for everyone, but people describe things like:
- anxiety, trouble sleeping, a racing "what now" mind
- feeling useless, unneeded, or like your skills don't matter anymore
- losing a sense of who you are when the work goes
- pulling away from people, going quiet

None of that means something is wrong *with you*. It's a human response to the ground shifting.

## What actually helps
This isn't therapy or medical advice — just honest, simple things that help people:
- **Name it.** Putting words to the feeling ("this is AIRD") takes some of its power away.
- **Know you're not alone.** This is a wave, not a personal failing.
- **One small step.** Not a five-year plan — just the next small thing today.
- **Tell one person.** The feeling shrinks the moment it's shared, even with a stranger.
- **Talk to a professional if it's heavy.** Naming it doesn't replace real help when you need it.

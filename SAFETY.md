# Kindred — Safety Posture (v0.1)

**One line:** Kindred is **non-clinical, AI-assisted, human-reviewed** outreach and navigation. It helps communities notice and gently support people who are quietly isolating — *earlier*, and *with consent*. It is **not** therapy, **not** diagnosis, and **not** crisis automation.

## What Kindred is / is not

| Kindred **is** | Kindred is **not** |
|---|---|
| Feeling-first content that helps people feel seen | A therapist, counsellor, or clinician |
| A low-friction, optional first point of contact | A diagnosis, assessment, or screening tool |
| AI-*assisted*, **human-reviewed** support | Autonomous AI messaging vulnerable people |
| A bridge **to** existing help | A replacement for crisis lines or emergency care |
| A way to measure unmet need ethically | A surveillance, scoring, or risk-prediction system |

## Red lines (we do not do these)
- ❌ No diagnosis, therapy, clinical triage, or **suicide-risk prediction/scoring**.
- ❌ No **autonomous outreach** to identifiable individuals without consent and a trusted-human path.
- ❌ No reporting of identifiable mental-health status to **employers, schools, or third parties**.
- ❌ No claims of clinical efficacy or medical advice; no pretending an AI is a human professional.
- ❌ No collecting data we don't need; no selling or ad-targeting of user data, ever.

## Human-in-the-loop
- AI **drafts and surfaces**; a trained human **reviews before anything sensitive reaches a person**.
- Every AI interaction is clearly labelled as AI. Users can reach a human and opt out instantly.
- Outreach is **broadcast / opt-in by design** (people come to the content), not targeted DMs to isolated people.

## Crisis handling (routing, not triage)
- Kindred **does not assess or manage crisis risk**. If a person signals immediate danger, the only job is to **route to professional crisis resources** (e.g., **9-8-8** Suicide Crisis Helpline in Canada & US; local emergency services) with a clear, always-visible path.
- A **written escalation workflow** exists before any user-facing pilot: detect distress signal → show crisis resources → hand to trained human → log (de-identified) for safety review.

## Consent & privacy (defaults)
- **Opt-in, anonymous by default.** No real identity required to receive support.
- **Data minimization:** collect the least possible; short retention; de-identify for any research.
- Plain-language consent; easy withdrawal; no dark patterns. Aligned with **PIPEDA** (Canada).

## AI / model guardrails
- Prompts and filters that **refuse clinical/medical advice** and **redirect to human/crisis resources** on risk signals.
- De-identified, auditable interaction logs for safety review.
- Regular checks for harm, bias, and wrong escalation/de-escalation.

## Governance & accountability
- **Lived-experience + clinical/mental-health advisor** input from day one. No user-facing pilot without an advisor sign-off on the safety workflow.
- Open-source, so methods and guardrails can be inspected.
- Any safety event is logged, reviewed, and drives a guardrail update.

## Kill / pause criteria — we stop a path if it…
- requires diagnosis, therapy, triage, or risk prediction before clinical governance exists;
- involves automated outreach without consent or a trusted-human path;
- has no written crisis-escalation, privacy, and moderation workflow in place;
- would report identifiable mental-health status to an employer/school/third party.

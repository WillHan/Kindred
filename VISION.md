# Kindred — Vision & Background

> **Note (2026-06):** This is the original concept draft, kept for the thinking behind Kindred. Where it talks about "proactive AI outreach" that *identifies* individuals, the project has since adopted an **opt-in, human-in-the-loop** approach: people come to Kindred, and we never target, score, or message individuals without consent. See [SAFETY.md](./SAFETY.md). The vision below is preserved; the *method* is governed by the safety posture.

---

## The Problem: The Hidden Crisis of AI Job Displacement

We are experiencing an "occupational identity crisis" as AI reshapes industries and the way people define purpose in their careers.

**The psychological toll:** Workers whose skills are being automated suffer from what we call "AI Replacement Dysfunction" (AIRD) — anxiety, insomnia, loss of identity, and feelings of worthlessness. Tech professionals in particular experience "identity grief" as they watch their core skills become automated faster than they can adapt.

**The institutional gap:** There is no established cultural vocabulary, HR framework, or institutional support structure designed to handle this specific type of AI-driven displacement.

## Mission

Kindred is a community-driven, open-source initiative that uses AI to help workers affected by AI-induced displacement, and to deliver supportive, non-clinical guidance to help them transition into the post-labor economy — always opt-in, always with a human in the loop.

## How it works

**Phase 1 — Meeting people where they are (opt-in).**
Kindred creates feeling-first content for people facing serial job loss and chronic uncertainty, especially in highly exposed roles (customer service, translation, routine programming). Because many people read job loss as the world saying they are "no longer needed," our content and responses are built to be thoughtful and validating. People choose to engage; a trained human stays in the loop.

**Phase 2 — Supportive, non-clinical roadmaps.**
Once someone chooses to engage, Kindred can help them think through three areas — never as therapy or diagnosis, always as supportive navigation toward real help:

- **Psychological resilience & cognitive flexibility:** helping people separate their personal identity from a job title, so they are less shaken by market disruption (self-concept clarity).
- **Strategic upskilling:** pointing toward "reinstatement" roles where humans still have an edge — emotional intelligence, physical dexterity, complex human interaction — and habits that avoid over-relying on AI for thinking.
- **Economic transition:** sharing emerging models for the post-labor economy (e.g., fractional ownership in AI systems, micro-royalties for training-data contributions, Universal Basic Compute).

## Why a non-profit, AI-first approach
A profit-driven company can't make money serving the people who go quiet, and existing services wait for people to reach out. AI, for the first time, makes it cheap to reach and listen to these people early — before the harm becomes permanent.
